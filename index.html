About Me

PhD Student passionate about advancing biomechanics and engineering solutions

I am a PhD student in Mechanical Engineering & Material Science at the University of Pittsburgh. My research focuses on the intersection of biomechanics and engineering, with particular emphasis on soft tissue mechanics, wave propagation phenomena, and non-destructive evaluation techniques.

My work contributes to the understanding of how mechanical waves interact with biological tissues, which has applications in medical diagnostics, structural health monitoring, and biomedical engineering.

Research

Exploring the frontiers of biomechanics and advanced materials

Soft Tissue Biomechanics

Investigation of mechanical properties and behavior of soft biological tissues under various loading conditions. This research contributes to understanding tissue response and developing better medical treatments.

Dielectric Elastomer Actuators

Development and characterization of smart materials that can change shape when subjected to electric fields. Applications include soft robotics, artificial muscles, and adaptive structures.

Non-Destructive Evaluation

Development of non-invasive techniques for evaluating material properties and detecting defects without causing damage to the specimen or structure.
