Electromechanical Performance of Dielectric Elastomer Composites: Modeling and Experimental Characterization

Ajay Kumar, Aman Khurana, Asesh Kumar Patra, Yamnesh Agrawal, MM Joglekar

Composite Structures, 320, 117130 (2023)

View on Journal Google Scholar

Abstract

This comprehensive study focuses on the electromechanical performance of dielectric elastomer composites through combined modeling and experimental approaches. Dielectric elastomer composites represent a promising class of smart materials that can undergo large deformations when subjected to electric fields, making them ideal for actuator applications.

The research integrates theoretical modeling with experimental characterization to understand the complex electromechanical coupling in these composite systems. The work provides valuable insights into optimizing the performance of dielectric elastomer composites for various engineering applications.

Research Objectives

  • Develop comprehensive electromechanical models for dielectric elastomer composites
  • Conduct experimental characterization of material properties
  • Validate theoretical predictions with experimental data
  • Identify optimal composite configurations for actuator applications

Methodology

The research methodology combined:

  • Theoretical modeling of electromechanical coupling
  • Finite element analysis of composite structures
  • Experimental testing of material samples
  • Performance optimization studies

Key Findings

The study revealed important insights into the electromechanical behavior of dielectric elastomer composites:

  • Strong correlation between theoretical models and experimental results
  • Optimization strategies for enhanced actuator performance
  • Material property relationships for composite design
  • Performance metrics for practical applications

Conclusions

This work provides a comprehensive understanding of dielectric elastomer composites through integrated modeling and experimental approaches. The findings contribute to the development of more efficient and reliable smart material systems for actuator applications.

The validated models can be used for designing optimized dielectric elastomer composite systems, while the experimental methodologies provide standardized approaches for material characterization in this field.
